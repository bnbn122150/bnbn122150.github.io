Autobiography

My name is Yu-Jong Wang, but my friends on campus call me Victor.

In high school I was in an English-French experimental class, thus I received better training in English than other high school students and was introduced to basic French and some French culture. The English training I received in high school was quite challenging in all aspects: we were taught how to give speeches (and I was the English speech contest champion), we watched English films with no subtitles, we read Shakespeare, we had English stage plays. It was where I was equipped with the English skills I have now, and the reason why I applied for the Department of Foreign Languages and Literature.

In terms of study, I found literature classes easier to pass but linguistics classes more interesting in general. My favorite literature class is Study of poetry, and for linguistics I am especially interested in phonetics and phonology, which focus on the sound system of languages. My interest in how languages sound does not only affect my enrollment choices; when preparing for the senior play of the department, it also helps me pay more attention to how my characters should sound like.

Overall, I would say the education and training I received in college so far has not been very useful in helping me find a job in Taiwan in the future; but even if I could go back in time and choose again, I will still cease the opportunity to obtain knowledge of what I like. Currently I am preparing for a test for a position in the Immigration Agency, which is a job in which my English skills would come in handy.
